Installation Guide

To begin your journey in using Zorin OS, you will need to do a few things first:

  • Visit the Zorin OS website and click on "Download Zorin OS."
  • Scroll down and click on "Download" under the Zorin OS Core section.
  • Go to the Balena Etcher website and click "Download" for your system.
  • Ensure you have a USB drive with at least 4GB of storage.

Once you have downloaded the ISO file you need to create a bootable USB drive to have your computer boot into the Zorin OS. To do that you need to flash the ISO file into the USB drive using the Balena Etcher software.

Once you have successfully created the bootable USB using Balena Etcher, turn off your computer and boot into the BIOS or UEFI settings, typically by pressing keys like F2, F12, or Del when your computer starts. In the settings, navigate to the Boot menu and select your USB drive as the first boot device. Save your changes and exit; your computer should now boot directly from the Zorin OS USB.

When you are booting from your USB drive, you are first greeted by a welcome page with two options: "Try Zorin OS" and "Install Zorin OS." If you want to have a feel for Zorin OS before buying it, then you can simply choose "Try Zorin OS," and otherwise select "Install Zorin OS" to proceed immediately with the setup.

Next, you will be prompted to choose your language, a specific keyboard layout, and network settings such that the computer is able to connect to the internet. After you have established these settings, you will select an installation option. For most users, "Erase disk and install Zorin OS" is ideal as it will provide you with a clean installation. But if you are an experienced user or you want to dual-boot another OS (e.g., Windows), select "Something else," and you will get to do your partitions by hand. It will then begin copying files to your hard drive. Meanwhile, you will get a chance to choose your geographical location for purposes of time-zones and to set up your user account, your username, password, and computer name.

As soon as you complete the installation, you will be asked to restart your computer. Remove the USB drive while your computer is restarting to avoid booting in again. You will then greet the clean and welcoming Zorin OS login screen on booting again after the reboot.

Post-Installation Setup

Now that you've managed to successfully install Zorin OS onto your device, you can now go ahead and customize your system to your liking. You can rearrange your desktop layout, install additional software from the Zorin OS Store, and tweak system settings to fit your workflow for whatever you intend to use it for. There are just too many things that Zorin OS has to help you personalize your desktop to your own uniqueness, from changing themes to shortcuts.

Troubleshooting & Tips

If you do find yourself having trouble, don't worry. There are open forums on the official Zorin website and a troubleshooting section where common issues are dealt with and solutions given to set you straight. From drivers installation to software updates, there are also plentiful tips on upgrading your Zorin OS available.
